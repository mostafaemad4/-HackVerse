Hacker Risks

Hackers pose a serious threat to individuals and organizations, and the most prominent risks they pose are:

Personal data breach: Stealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank card data, and personal identities.

Cyber attacks on organizations: Hacking into corporate systems and disrupting operations. Stealing corporate secrets and customer data: Carrying out ransomware attacks where data is encrypted and a ransom is demanded to decrypt it.

Electronic fraud: Using fake email or fake websites to deceive victims. And carrying out fraudulent operations such as phishing and stealing money online.

Electronic espionage: Collecting sensitive information about individuals or countries for intelligence or commercial purposes.

Spreading malware: Using viruses, Trojans, and malicious software to hack systems and destroy data. Disabling devices through attacks such as DDoS that flood servers with requests.

Hacking smart devices: Targeting smartphones and internet-connected devices to steal data or spy on them..

How does the hacking process happen?

The hacker uses one of the following methods to hack :

Security vulnerabilities in software or servers can give a hacker the opportunity to gain unauthorized access to a site or system. Or phishing, where hackers send fake emails or messages that appear to be from a trusted source (such as banks or well-known companies). The goal is to trick a person into entering sensitive information such as passwords or credit card numbers on fake pages that look like the original sites. There are other methods such as hackers who try to guess passwords by trying many possible combinations using special programs. When passwords are weak or short, it is easy for a hacker to access accounts. Or Malware such as viruses, worms or Trojans can be installed on the victim's device by downloading files attached to emails or by downloading infected programs. Once the malware is installed, the hacker can take control of the victim's device or steal data. Or through open Wi-Fi networks,they may exploit public Wi-Fi networks to access data sent between devices, allowing them to eavesdrop on users' conversations or steal login data and sensitive information..

The hacker's goals from the hacking process he carries out..

There are many goals that hackers hack websites and devices for :

The hacker's goals from hacking operations are to access sensitive information such as bank account data, passwords, identity data, or personal information. Spying and monitoring by collecting information about individuals, companies, or governments for commercial or political purposes. Achieving financial gain by hacking bank accounts or credit cards and withdrawing money. There are other goals such as disrupting the systems of companies or institutions as a form of revenge or to achieve ideological purposes.

How to protect yourself from hacking?

There are several ways to protect yourself from hacking, including :

Protect your passwords by using strong passwords that contain upper and lower case letters, numbers and symbols. Avoid using common passwords or personal information such as your date of birth. Do not use the same password for all accounts. Be careful with links and attachments Avoid clicking on links or opening attachments sent from unknown sources. Avoid using public networks to exchange sensitive information. Avoid downloading untrusted programs

* Hacking can happen due to simple negligence, so treat everything on the Internet with caution and stay informed of the latest cyber threats.
